We bridge the gap in understanding the needs and experiences of BIPOC and/or LGBTQIA+ people

Chosen Collective Consulting, LLC is an education and community centered consulting firm that supports organizations in shifting towards a climate of equity, justice and inclusion while intentionally centering care and support for BIPOC and/or LGBTQIA+ folks. Our team is made up of experts in fields of educational access, social work, counseling and therapy,artistic development, curriculum development, critical race theory, LGBTQIA+ inclusion, organizational development and more.

We promote the value and knowledge fostered by lived experiences that are often marginalized or structurally excluded in organizational environments. Chosen also hosts yearly, “Reset-Retreats", a travel retreat space for folks of color, and/or LGBTQIA+ folks who do community care-centered and organizing work.

Our Story

Co-founders, Dio Aldridge and Kristen Surla met as Assistant Directos at Oberlin College's Multicultural Resource Center where they led intersectional-focused programs and coalition building across LGBTQIA+, BIPOC, immigrant, and first-generation college student communities.

Dio and Kris intentionally cultivate spaces for community building, storytelling, and advocacy for BIPOC and LGBTQIA+ populations—oftentimes these spaces evolved from "chosen family" gatherings. What made these spaces unique was the pairing of DEIJ advocacy with wellness practices. Chosen Collective beleives that social justice work is inherently tied to healing and community empowerment

About Dio

Hailing from strong Afro-Caribbean and Latinx roots, Dio Aldridge, pronouns he/él, is a Blatinx Trans man born in Limón, Costa Rica and raised in Southern California. Growing up in the household of a Baptist minister and Social Worker proved to be formative in shaping his passion and work in community building, engagement, organizational development, education and art.

Dio has spent over 9 years in the field of diversity, equity, and inclusion education and organizational development and is the Co- Founder & CEO of Chosen Collective Consulting & Reset-Retreats, LLC. Chosen, co-founded with his partner Kristen Surla, MeD. Chosen supports organizations in shifting towards a climate of equity, justice and inclusion while intentionally centering wellness and care for BIPOC and/or LGBTQIA+ folks by delivering strategic planning, organizational workshops and hosting international reset-retreats. Dio welcomes the opportunity to engage in difficult, transformative, and necessary dialogue and believes that dialogue must be matched with a plan and action. Dio also believes in a good nap.

Dio earned his Bachelors of Arts in Black Studies from UC Santa Barbara and a Dual Masters in Social Work & Social Justice with a specialization in leadership and development in social services from Loyola University Chicago.

About Kris

Kristen Surla (she/her/siya) is a DEI Training and Curriculum Specialist focused on faculty and staff development at the School for Environment and Sustainability at the University of Michigan. She is currently finishing her PhD in the Higher, Adult, and Lifelong Education Program at Michigan State University. Her dissertation research interrogates the navigation of academic socialization, funding structures, and identity capitalism by BIPOC graduate students in science fields.

Kris also infuses art into all aspects of her life including community engagement, teaching, and wellness. She believes in the power of art making as a healing practice that encourages deep reflection and more expansive modes of communication.

About Lucie

Lucie Ross (she/her/ella) is a multi-disciplinary artist and teacher from Argentina, specializing in the fields of circus and performing arts, graduated from the National Circus School of Montreal. She has been traveling the world for over 12 years performing, teaching, competing, and training, which has given Lucie the gift of experiencing life surrounded by people from many different backgrounds and cultures, living in and visiting places such as Chicago, Germany, Abu Dhabi, China, Japan, South Korea, and Canada. She is passionate about the infinite ways in which humans connect, understand and accept themselves and each other, which inspires her work as an acrobat, a dancer, a yogi, and in all spheres in which one can show up as a person.

Lucie is a curious being interested in learning about the mechanisms that exist within our planet, our societies, and ourselves, encouraging her to explore varied subjects like sustainability, neurobiology, meditation, social justice, music, health, nutrition, gardening, visual arts, and many more.

Lucie joined Chosen Collective in March 2021 as a Communications & Administrative Manager. She is excited to dive into this new territory of work for her and support Chosen Collective, its mission and values to the best of her ability.

Lucie is currently studying Dance-Theater at the National University of Arts (U.N.A.) in Argentina, and she is also pursuing a yoga teacher training at the Institute of Yoga Darshana San Isidro. She regularly teaches group and private online workshops in Physical Training and Movement.

About Mel

Mel is a Queer Latinx creative born and raised in Chicago, Illinois with a passion for learning and understanding people, narratives, structures, and the overall way the world eb and flows around us. Mel is an illustrator whose work often explores memory, how and why certain memories stick to us, despite how big or small they may seem in the moment of experiencing them, and how they shape the people we grow into.

Mel believes that centering equity, inclusion, and identity is essential to uplifting voices for communities who have not been given full rights, access. This means that we must have a voice and be a part of shaping and envisioning a more equitable future.

About Ali

Ali Guajardo, LCSW (he/him) is a behavioral health consultant and clinician at the Santa Barbara Neighborhood Clinics where he specializes in transgender behavioral healthcare. With over six years experience serving Santa Barbara's BIPOC communities and as a former foster youth, Ali weaves experiential knowledge with clinical expertise to offer intersectional approaches to his practice. Ali's work is inspired by the powerful people he's witnessed struggle against impossible systems including family members, both bio and chosen.

About Art

Portfolio: Art Phung

Art is a digital designer who is passionate about making people’s lives easier through the powers of design. Their belief is that most problems can be solved if creators center people who are marginalized and oftentimes overlooked in the design process. They bring more than 7 years of knowledge in diversity, equity, and inclusion incorporated into their experience in design thinking. About Shá

Shá currently works in the Office of Equity at Northwestern University, with eight years of prior experience in non-profit management and community organizing experience. Their speciality is in cultivating and facilitate dialogue and behavior based workshops centering anti-racist and anti-oppression, harm reduction and prevention. Further, they also facilitate workshops in the area of healthy sexuality in student groups and organizations.

Prior to Northwestern position they taught performing arts and creative writing all across Chicago through the theater based non-profit, Barrel of Monkeys. This experience made aware of the deep inequities in educational systems for students from marginalized backgrounds. This led to their passion for not only the work of advocacy for equity in education, but also the training and professional development of administrators, staff, and program instructors around equitable educational practices and cultural competencies.

They have worked with prominent non-profits including Youth Guidance, Youth Opportunity United in Evanston, and recently served as Interim Executive Director of PlayMakers Laboratory. Shá holds their BA in Communications from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte and Masters of Education in Higher Education from Loyola University of Chicago.
