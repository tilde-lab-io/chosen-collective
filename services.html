Our Committment

Featured Workshops

Leadership & Employee Resource Group Coaching

Chosen Collective is proud to offer 3 or 6 month employee resource group coaching to support the successful launch and/or improved impact of current Employee Resource Groups (ERGS) for underrepresented groups within your organization.This includes Black, Latinx, Women’s, Asian Pacific Islander Diaspora, LGBTQIA+ and disability/accessibility. Our holistic coaching ensures the wellness of group leaders and the successful development of goals, vision, implementation strategy and support

Cultivating a Gender Affirming Workplace & Client Culture

Learning about the needs and nuanced experiences of gender expansive people will cultivate an inclusive employee environment and client experience. This workshop will engage in a deeper understanding of the day to day realities and issues that shape of transgender and non-binary experiences. Through definitions, activities and trans equity frameworks, participants will leave with tools to cultivate gender affirming community practices, policy considerations, and culture

Practices for Solidarity & Allyship during Juneteenth and Pride

In June, two significant holidays are recognized for the experiences and legacies of their communities: Juneteenth and Pride month. Chosen Collective encourages people to honor both Black and LGBTQIA+ communities throughout June by reviewing historical and contemporary examples which connect them. This workshop reviews practices of self-education, solidarity building, and intersectional allyship-- three critical tools essential for supporting increasingly diverse populations in our workplaces, families, and communities. Participants will receive a historical overview of events and leaders who have advocated for LGBTQIA+ and Black rights in tandem, rather than as separate movements.

Understanding Self & Building a Community of Care

Self-awareness is key to contributing towards a community that values and takes action towards equity, inclusion and addressing microaggressions. This workshop takes participants through self-questionnaires to further understand needs for communication, community building, feedback and more. Participants then learn the different types of microaggressions and their impact on underrepresented people and leave with language for addressing concerns with colleagues and community members. At the end of this workshop colleagues better understand self, each other and have strategies for cultivating a community of care.
